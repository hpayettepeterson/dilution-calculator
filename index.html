How the protection modes work

Post-money cap: the stake is shielded from dilution in any round whose post-money valuation does not exceed the cap. The first round whose post-money exceeds the cap, and every round after, dilutes normally.

Cumulative raised cap: protection holds while total equity raised (after the round closes) stays at or below the cap. The first round that pushes cumulative raised above the cap is the first to dilute.

Through round: the stake is protected up to and including the selected round; all later rounds dilute normally.

Equivalent fully-dilutable stake: the starting % of an ordinary, fully-dilutable stake that would land on the same final % after all rounds. It is how much ordinary equity the protection is "worth" at incorporation.
